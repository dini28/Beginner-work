Great Indian Mathematicians

India has produced some of the world's most brilliant mathematical minds. Their contributions have shaped modern mathematics and continue to inspire generations of scholars.

Aryabhata (476-550 CE)

Known as the father of Indian mathematics and astronomy. He introduced the concept of zero, calculated the value of π (pi), and developed the place-value system. His work "Aryabhatiya" is one of the earliest surviving mathematical texts.

  • Introduced the decimal place-value system
  • Calculated π = 3.1416
  • Developed trigonometric functions
  • Solved quadratic equations

Srinivasa Ramanujan (1887-1920)

A self-taught mathematical genius who made extraordinary contributions to mathematical analysis, number theory, infinite series, and continued fractions. His notebooks contain thousands of mathematical results.

  • Discovered over 3,900 mathematical results
  • Developed the Ramanujan prime and Ramanujan theta function
  • Contributed to partition theory
  • Worked on mock theta functions

Shakuntala Devi (1929-2013)

Known as the "Human Computer," she demonstrated extraordinary mental calculation abilities. She could perform complex calculations faster than electronic computers of her time.

  • Calculated 23rd root of a 201-digit number in 50 seconds
  • Multiplied two 13-digit numbers in 28 seconds
  • Authored several books on mathematics
  • Promoted mathematical education worldwide

Brahmagupta (628-668 CE)

First mathematician to give rules for computing with zero and negative numbers. He developed the formula for the area of a cyclic quadrilateral and made significant contributions to algebra.

  • Established rules for zero and negative numbers
  • Developed Brahmagupta's formula for cyclic quadrilaterals
  • Solved quadratic equations
  • Contributed to number theory

Bhaskara II (1114-1185 CE)

Also known as Bhaskaracharya, he made significant contributions to algebra, arithmetic, geometry, and astronomy. His work "Lilavati" is famous for its mathematical problems.

  • Developed concepts of infinitesimal calculus
  • Solved Pell's equation
  • Calculated planetary positions accurately
  • Contributed to combinatorics

Madhava of Sangamagrama (1340-1425 CE)

Considered the founder of mathematical analysis. He developed infinite series expansions for trigonometric functions, centuries before Newton and Leibniz.

  • Developed Taylor series for trigonometric functions
  • Calculated π to 11 decimal places
  • Founded the Kerala School of Mathematics
  • Contributed to calculus concepts

Mathematical Calculation Rules

Understanding the fundamental rules and principles that govern mathematical calculations.

Basic Arithmetic Rules

  • Order of Operations (PEMDAS/BODMAS): Parentheses/Brackets, Exponents/Orders, Multiplication and Division (left to right), Addition and Subtraction (left to right)
  • Commutative Property: a + b = b + a, a × b = b × a
  • Associative Property: (a + b) + c = a + (b + c), (a × b) × c = a × (b × c)
  • Distributive Property: a × (b + c) = a × b + a × c

Trigonometric Rules

  • Pythagorean Identity: sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
  • Angle Addition: sin(A + B) = sin(A)cos(B) + cos(A)sin(B)
  • Double Angle: sin(2θ) = 2sin(θ)cos(θ)
  • Unit Circle: sin(0°) = 0, cos(0°) = 1, sin(90°) = 1, cos(90°) = 0

Logarithmic Rules

  • Product Rule: log(ab) = log(a) + log(b)
  • Quotient Rule: log(a/b) = log(a) - log(b)
  • Power Rule: log(a^n) = n × log(a)
  • Change of Base: log_b(a) = log(a) / log(b)

Algebraic Rules

  • Exponent Rules: a^m × a^n = a^(m+n), (a^m)^n = a^(mn)
  • Radical Rules: √(ab) = √a × √b, √(a/b) = √a / √b
  • Factoring: a² - b² = (a + b)(a - b)
  • Quadratic Formula: x = (-b ± √(b² - 4ac)) / 2a
